# Head of Ecosystem Relations — Role Plan
**Title:** Head of Ecosystem Relations (HoER)
**Organisation:** EEZ Association
**Date:** April 2026
**Status:** Active

---

## Purpose

EEZ's only asset before mainnet is credibility. HoER is the function that builds, protects, and deploys that credibility through content, brand, and Alliance coordination.

The role has one core output: a market that hears "EEZ" and thinks "credible, neutral, technically real" — not "Gnosis product." Everything HoER does either advances or defends that output.

---

## What HoER Owns

### Brand & Positioning
- **Messaging guide** — the single approved document for category framing, approved/forbidden language, and segment-specific variants. Maintained and versioned.
- **Positioning guardian** — reviews all public content before publication. The last line of defence against off-message framing, unverified claims, and EF Interop Layer conflation.
- **Governance language sheet** — the approved Swiss non-profit language every Alliance member must use before publishing. Non-negotiable inclusion in every content brief.
- **Brand standards enforcement** — rejects or returns any content that misrepresents EEZ's governance, neutrality, or technical claims.

### Content
- Alliance content brief and draft review
- Alliance publication calendar (the coordinated simultaneous window)
- Alliance member spotlight series (Phase 2)
- eez.io website copy — /alliance, /governance, /docs landing, homepage
- @etheconomiczone social content — writes or approves all posts
- EthResearch native posts — peer-participation tone, not marketing
- Podcast and media content prep — talking points and framing for Ernst and Baylina
- Press and earned media pitching — narrative angle, coordinates with Gnosis Comms
- Co-marketing with protocol launches

### Alliance Member Relations
- Named contact for all 10 founding members
- Content commitment extraction and publication coordination
- Member onboarding (EGL closes new members; HoER onboards)
- Alliance summit content, speakers, narrative (logistics joint with EGL)

### Reporting
- Weekly comms status to EEZ leadership: content in review, approved, published, off-message incidents caught
- Alliance member engagement tracker: last contact, content status, engagement level (active / drifting / at risk)

## 90-Day Plan

### Phase 1 — Technical Credibility + Alliance Activation (April–May 2026)

HoER's primary Phase 1 deliverable is the Alliance content sprint: 6–8 coordinated posts from founding members published in a simultaneous 10-day window. The timing is the strategy — simultaneous publication signals momentum; spread posts signal mild interest.

#### Week 1 (Apr 6–12): Build the Foundation

| Task | Output | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Map all 10 Alliance members | Champion name, personal email, last contact date, content readiness (spec-dependent or ready-now) | Apr 7 |
| Finalise messaging guide | Approved category framing, forbidden language list, segment variants | Apr 8 |
| Write governance language sheet | Approved Swiss non-profit language block for member posts | Apr 8 |
| Write and send first outreach email | 10 personal emails sent — specific ask included (not after the call, in the email) | Apr 9 |
| Write Alliance content brief | 300–600 word "why we joined" brief with format, deadline, governance language requirement | Apr 9 |
| Segment members | Spec-dependent vs. ready-now list — activate ready-now members immediately | Apr 9 |

**Do not wait for the call to make the ask. The ask goes in the first email.**

#### Week 2 (Apr 13–19): Activation Calls

| Task | Output | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Conduct 30-min calls with all 10 members | Written commitment (yes / no / timeline) from each | Apr 19 |
| Distribute content brief to all committed members | Brief received, deadline confirmed | Apr 19 |
| Set up shared content calendar | Single tracker: member name, format, draft due date, publish date | Apr 16 |
| Begin draft review for fast-movers | 2–3 drafts in review | Apr 19 |

**Call agenda (30 minutes, strict):** (1) What are you expecting from EEZ membership in the next 90 days? (2) Can you commit to a post by [date]? (3) What do you need from us to write it? Get the commitment or surface the blocker. No pitch. No over-explaining.

#### Week 3 (Apr 20–26): Draft Review Sprint

| Task | Output | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Review all incoming drafts | Approve or return with specific edits | Rolling |
| Governance language audit on every draft | Zero "Gnosis product" or "Gnosis interoperability layer" language | Rolling |
| Send Spec Direction Preview to spec-dependent members | Members stay engaged without needing the full spec | May 1 |
| Check leading indicator: 5+ drafts in queue | Green = proceed / Red = escalate immediately | Apr 28 |

#### Week 4 (Apr 27–May 3): Lock the Sprint

| Task | Output | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Confirm publication window | Every committed member has a publish date in the coordinated 10-day window | Apr 30 |
| Align with Gnosis Comms on @etheconomiczone amplification | Reshare plan ready for each member post | Apr 30 |
| **Leading indicator gate: 5+ committed drafts in queue** | Green → Phase 1 on track / Red → escalate, narrow sprint to highest-credibility members | **Apr 28** |
| Begin eez.io copy audit | /alliance, /governance pages reviewed and updated | May 3 |

#### May: Sprint Execution + Phase Gate

| Task | Output | Deadline |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Execute coordinated publication window | 6–8 posts live in 10-day window | Mid-May |
| @etheconomiczone reshares with commentary | Each member post amplified | Concurrent |
| Alliance member engagement tracker updated | Post-sprint engagement level per member | May 20 |
| **Phase 1 gate check** | Alliance sprint complete + benchmark validated + Re-org FAQ published | End of May |

**Phase 1 gate (end of May):** Alliance content sprint complete · Benchmark validated (Zisk/Baylina, Apr 15) · Re-org FAQ published (DevRel, Apr 20). All three required. If spec slips past June, run the credibility track independently — do not gate the sprint on spec.

### Phase 2 — Ecosystem Adoption (May–July 2026)

Phase 2 assumes spec is published and EGL is in role. HoER shifts from sprint coordination to sustained content production and member deepening.

| Task | Cadence | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Alliance member spotlight series | One per week | Profile the founding member: specific use case, technical depth, what composability unlocks for their users |
| @etheconomiczone content | 3–5x per week | Milestone amplification, member reshares with commentary, technical threads. No teaser posts. |
| EthResearch native post | Phase 2: one follow-up post | Integration learnings from first protocol deployment. Native tone — not a press release. |
| Podcast and media prep | On-demand | Brief Ernst / Baylina before each appearance. Approve framing. No appearances before spec is published. |
| Press and earned media | Milestone-driven | Pitch real news — first protocol integration, first non-Gnosis L2. Not "exciting update" announcements. |
| Co-marketing with first protocol launch | On launch | Brief, draft, review, publish joint content with the launching protocol. |
| eez.io website updates | On milestone | /alliance updated with new members and spotlight links. |
| Alliance member engagement review | Monthly | Update tracker: active / drifting / at risk. Re-engage drifting members with a new specific ask. |

---

### Phase 3 — Developer Ecosystem (Q3 2026)

Phase 3 gates on: 2+ EEZ rollups in production + major protocol deployed via composability + UX demonstrable to non-technical user.

| Task | Notes |
| :--- | :--- |
| Alliance summit (joint with EGL) | HoER owns: content agenda, speaker selection, narrative framing, post-event content. EGL owns: logistics, invitations, venue. |
| Co-marketing with first major composability protocol launch | Full content campaign: brief, assets, coordinated publication. |
| Alliance spotlight series continues | Pivot to post-integration stories — "what actually changed for our users" |
| Messaging guide update | Refresh for Phase 3 framing: from "here's why EEZ" to "here's what EEZ enabled" |

## Red Flags and Escalation

| Signal | What It Means | Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| <3 Alliance drafts in queue by Apr 28 | Sprint is failing — not enough commitment extracted | Escalate to Ernst for direct founder-to-founder ask on Aave, Safe, Flashbots |
| Any member post goes live without HoER review | Message risk — "Gnosis product" framing may be published | Request retraction or correction immediately. Audit all remaining drafts. |
| Alliance member stops responding | Member at risk of going passive | Re-engage with a new, smaller ask. If no response in 2 weeks, flag to EGL for CRM update. |
| Spec slips past June | Phase 1 gate dependency breaks | Decouple the Alliance sprint from spec. Activate ready-now members on sovereignty/governance narratives immediately. |
| Non-Gnosis L2 pipeline shows 0 qualified conversations by Apr 21 | Phase 2 gate at risk | Flag to EEZ leadership. Ernst to make direct founder-to-founder contact — do not route through HoER. |
